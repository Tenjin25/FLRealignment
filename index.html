Miami-Dade County - Urban Shift

Trend: Once a reliable Democratic stronghold, Miami-Dade has become increasingly competitive in recent elections.

Key Factor: Growing Cuban-American and Venezuelan-American populations have shifted voting patterns, particularly in congressional races.

Geography: Urban core remains Democratic while suburbs show Republican gains.

Orange County - Suburban Evolution

Trend: Orange County has transitioned from swing county to Democratic-leaning due to rapid population growth and demographic changes.

Key Factor: Orlando metro expansion and younger, more diverse voter registration.

Impact: Critical to statewide Democratic competitiveness.

Statewide Patterns

Rural-Urban Divide: Growing polarization between urban coastal counties and rural interior counties.

I-4 Corridor: Remains the key battleground determining statewide outcomes.

Registration Trends: Unaffiliated voter growth creating volatile election dynamics.
